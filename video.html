Hyrule is a vast and beautiful landscape full of different places to discover. Hyrule has long been known for its varied landscape and large stunning vistas. From Hyrule Castle, in the center of the mainland, to the Gerudo Desert in the south west, there is countless things to discover.

Your trip will take you all over Hyrule, and you'll get to experience the various cultures and monuments within.

Visit Kakarike Village

Kakariko Village is one of the main villages in centeral Hyrule. Known for its hospitality and deep cultural roots, your stay here will be nothing short of transformative.

The village is nestled in a small canyon full of waterfalls, trees, and maybe even a Cucco or two!

Picnic at Lake Hylia

Lake Hylia is located only an hour from the main castle and is one of the staples of Hyrule. Many woodland and aquatic creatures call this lake home so there's always plenty to be seen at the lake. If you're lucky, on full moons and at certain time of the night people have said they can spot a large dragon coiling through the sky near the bridge.

Discover Death Mountain the hottest place in Hyrule

Death Mountain is something you just can't miss! Located at the Nothern most tip of Hyrule, it is one of the largest mountains in Hyrule. No matter where you are, you can always see Death Mountain, especially at night when the peak of the mountain glows a bright orange and red.

If you think you're brave enough to try and climb Death Mountain, a guided adventure hike lead by a team of experienced Goron, will take you up the sweltering hot paths of Death Mountain.
